Data Driven Criminal Justice

Surveys on the needs, challenges and variables of developing a data driven criminal justice system

The GovLab is leading a year-long project, funded by the John and Laura Arnold Foundation, and in close collaboration with the office of the U.S. Chief Data Scientist at the White House, to analyze and document how data flows through the criminal justice system. The aim is to identify policy and legal barriers to effective data sharing among agencies and between agencies and researchers.

To date the GovLab is conducting a series of ongoing surveys designed to provide a more complete picture and help us understanding of how criminal justice communities use data and where there is a need for more information and training, as well as a survey of empirical criminal justice researchers.

Imagine if...

Imagine if we had data from one individual from the point of arrest to post-release: We could divert more people to alternative programs and tackle recidivism.

Imagine if...

Imagine if we we had data that could be responsibly shared among different agencies with efficient technological platforms and policies that enable confidential information on substance-abuse and mental disorders to be securely managed and used by criminal justice practitioners we could have a better understanding of who is cycling through multiple systems and predict who is likely to turn into a super-utilizer.

Imagine if...

Imagine if data from multiple jurisdictions we could have a better understanding what programs are working more efficiently, scaling and replicating those interventions that are proving to reduce mass incarceration and lower crime.
